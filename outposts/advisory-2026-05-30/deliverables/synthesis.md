# Strategic Brief: mojave Advisory Synthesis

**Date:** 2026-05-30
**Scope:** Synthesis of 11 advisory reports (5 wave-1, 6 wave-2) by 6 specialist agents

---

## What This Project Is Actually Trying to Do

mojave is building a metrological instrument for AI evaluation. Not a benchmark, not an eval framework, not a leaderboard -- an instrument in the sense that a calibrated gauge is an instrument: something whose own measurement properties are characterized, quantified, and traceable before it is trusted to measure anything else.

The project's thesis is that the current practice of LLM evaluation is not measurement. It is number assignment. Luca Mari's 2005 framework makes the distinction precise: measurement requires a declared measurement model, quantified uncertainty within that model, and traceability to reference standards. Contemporary benchmarks satisfy none of these requirements. They compute a proportion correct, report it to three decimal places, and treat it as though it were a physical quantity with meaning independent of how it was obtained. mojave exists because that treatment is wrong, and because the consequences of its wrongness scale with the stakes of the decisions made on its basis.

The project implements this thesis through five mathematical pillars in Rust: global sensitivity analysis (Sobol/Saltelli variance decomposition via salib-rs), sequential/anytime-valid inference (confidence sequences from Howard et al. 2021), statistical process control (Shewhart/CUSUM/EWMA/e-detector charts), inter-rater reliability (seven agreement statistics), and computerized adaptive testing (2PL IRT with game-theoretic item selection). These are wrapped in a tamper-evident audit chain (SHA-256 hash chain with Ed25519 attestation and a genesis sentinel binding each chain to a specific model identity), a Python eval pipeline built on UK AISI's Inspect framework, and LaTeX run cards that report the results. The codebase is approximately 30,000 lines of Rust across 17 workspace crates, with 77 Gherkin test specifications and a 4-gate validation methodology (textbook reproduction, R cross-checks, property-based tests, Monte Carlo calibration) that is unusual in its rigor for any software project, let alone one at research stage.

The target market is the defense establishment. The August 2026 DoD AI cybersecurity review mandate and the EU AI Act Article 73 serious-incident reporting deadline create immediate demand for precisely the kind of evaluation rigor mojave provides: lifecycle security, tamper protection, continuous monitoring, conformity assessment with quantified risk. No competing framework -- not UK AISI Inspect, not Spark-LLM-Eval, not OpenAI Evals, not DeepEval -- combines GSA, audit chains, and confidence sequences. mojave's competitive position is real, unique, and timely.

## What the State of the Field Says

The advisory's investigation reveals that mojave sits at the intersection of three measurement traditions that have never been unified for AI evaluation, and that each tradition validates a different aspect of what mojave is building.

**Metrology** (the science of measurement, as codified in the GUM and VIM). This tradition demands that every measurement report declare its measurement model, quantify all sources of uncertainty, and establish traceability. mojave's Sobol decomposition is a GUM Type B uncertainty evaluation: it quantifies how much of the observed variance comes from each identified source. mojave's confidence sequences are GUM Type A uncertainty evaluations: they quantify the statistical uncertainty from finite sampling. Together, they produce an uncertainty budget that is GUM-compliant in structure even though mojave does not yet label it as such. NIST AI 800-3, published in February 2026, independently calls for exactly this approach -- proposing GLMMs as a statistical framework for principled AI evaluation. mojave's Sobol decomposition generalizes NIST's GLMM variance decomposition to the experimental design setting. The institutional validation is already published and waiting to be cited.

**Psychometrics** (the science of psychological measurement, from Cronbach through Borsboom). This tradition demands construct validity: evidence that a test actually measures what it claims to measure. The advisory's most conceptually important finding is that Sobol first-order indices for construct-irrelevant factors are, under Borsboom's causal definition of validity, direct validity coefficients. If prompt template explains 85% of variance in WMDP scores, then WMDP's construct validity index for measuring hazardous knowledge is approximately 0.12. This is a devastating finding for WMDP -- and a proof of concept for mojave's value proposition. The perturbation engine is not just a sensitivity analysis tool; it is a construct validity testing engine. Every perturbation factor that drives significant variance quantifies invalidity. Generalizability theory (G-theory), which decomposes measurement variance across facets and produces reliability coefficients (G and Phi), is the psychometric tradition most directly aligned with mojave's Sobol decomposition. They are, mathematically, two notations for the same ANOVA decomposition applied to different domains. The quarantined G-theory implementation in salib-rs already does this computation.

**Defense measurement science** (QMU, ISO 5725, MSA/Gauge R&R, assurance cases). This is the tradition the advisory surfaced from outside the AI literature, and it is the one that transforms mojave from a research tool into a defense product. The nuclear weapons Stockpile Stewardship Program developed Quantification of Margins and Uncertainties (QMU) in the late 1990s to solve a problem structurally identical to mojave's: certifying system reliability when you cannot run the full test. QMU's Confidence Ratio -- margin divided by uncertainty -- composes all five of mojave's pillars into a single accept/reject/investigate decision. JCGM 106 guard-band decision rules formalize the accept/reject boundary with explicit consumer and producer risk. ISO 5725 interlaboratory comparison treats multi-configuration evaluation as a repeatability/reproducibility problem with formal outlier detection via Mandel h/k statistics. MSA/Gauge R&R asks whether the measurement system can actually discriminate between performance levels -- a question that IRR agreement statistics cannot answer. Goal Structuring Notation (GSN) assurance cases provide the defense-native reporting format that procurement evaluators, safety review boards, and accreditation authorities actually read.

None of these traditions has been applied to AI evaluation in the published literature. Sobol indices have been applied to neural network inputs (Fel et al. 2021, Sadeghi 2024) but never to evaluation pipeline variance. QMU has never been applied outside nuclear weapons, nuclear power, and waste repository assessment. The intersection is unoccupied. mojave is, in this precise sense, the first project to treat AI evaluation as an engineering measurement problem using the full toolkit that engineering measurement science provides.

## Three Things the Authors Don't Currently Know

**1. The confidence sequence pipeline is broken, and the tests don't catch it.**

The AnytimeMonitor -- the production code path for all sequential inference in mojave -- uses Welford's algorithm to estimate sigma from the sample, then feeds that estimate into Howard et al.'s normal-mixture confidence sequence formula. The formula's own docstring warns that this voids the anytime-valid guarantee. Monte Carlo verification confirms: empirical coverage is 46% at p=0.5 and 17% at p=0.1 (should be 95%). The Gate 4 test that appears to validate confidence sequences tests a different function -- `normal_mixture_cs_known_sigma` with the true sigma -- than the one actually used in production. This is not a theoretical concern; it means every confidence interval mojave has ever reported from the confseq pipeline is too narrow by approximately a factor of two. Additionally, the pipeline declares `DataFamily::Bernoulli` but routes binary MCQ data through Gaussian formulas. The BernoulliMonitor exists in the codebase and correctly implements the Beta-mixture mSPRT, but it returns no confidence interval. The eval-orchestrator's SequentialInstrument has the same defect through a different entry point. The fix is straightforward (sigma=0.5 as an immediate conservative bound; Waudby-Smith & Ramdas 2024 betting CS as the correct long-term solution), but the current state means the project's sequential inference claims are unsupported by the code.

**2. The headline WMDP finding is real but misleading, and the data has quality problems.**

"Prompt template explains 85% of variance" is technically correct but operationally vacuous. Approximately 60-70% of prompt template's apparent variance is driven by the "bare" template -- a pathological level that provides no format instructions, causing most models to produce unparseable output scored as accuracy=0.0. Among realistic prompt templates, prompt template likely explains 25-40% of variance. The finding "don't use a completely uninstructed prompt" is true but not useful. The finding "among production-quality prompts, prompt wording still drives a quarter to two-fifths of score variance" is useful but has not been reported. Additionally, 20 of 4,096 cells in the bio analysis have n_samples=0 coded as accuracy=0.0 -- these are infrastructure failures (RunPod timeouts), not genuine zero-accuracy outcomes, and they corrupt the Sobol estimator with 9 of the 20 concentrated in the quantization column-swap matrix. The plan's own convergence criterion ("if dominant-factor CI exceeds 10% of estimate, double N") was violated by 4.4x and no doubling was performed.

**3. The audit chain's trust model has a load-bearing gap, and the Python-Rust parity contract is broken.**

The audit chain provides genuine tamper evidence at the cryptographic level. The design choices are sound: domain-tagged hashing, float rejection in canonical encoding, AuditGate must-resolve enforcement, COSE_Sign1 attestation. But the trust model rests on an unstated assumption: that the binary producing the chain is authentic. Binary signing is listed as "REQUIRED BEFORE PRODUCTION" in FUTURE_WORK.md but is not implemented. Without it, the chain proves internal consistency of whatever the binary produced, but an adversary with write access to the host can replace the binary and produce valid-looking chains with fabricated data. For defense customers, this is the difference between a security control and a compliance artifact. Separately, the Python audit writer (audit.py) is format-incompatible with the Rust chain verifier: it lacks the tagged-union genesis sentinel format, the model identity binding, and the `"type"` discriminator field. Chains started by Python will fail Rust verification. The cross-language verification test silently skips when no compiled binary is found.

## Highest-Leverage Changes

**1. Adopt QMU as the top-level decision framework and build the 100-line integration layer.**

The QMU Confidence Ratio is a thin composition over existing primitives: margin (from confidence sequences) divided by expanded uncertainty (from confidence sequences), with the sensitivity profile (from Sobol) explaining what drives the margin and SPC charts confirming the margin is stable. This is approximately 100-200 lines of Rust. It produces a single accept/reject/investigate decision that defense procurement already understands. Combined with JCGM 106 guard-band decision rules (guarded acceptance with explicit consumer risk), this transforms mojave's output from "here are your statistics" to "the model does NOT pass under guarded acceptance with consumer risk < 5%." No competing framework has QMU, JCGM 106, or GSN assurance cases. This is structural differentiation.

The leverage is disproportionate because the mathematical novelty is zero -- it is pure composition -- but the strategic value is enormous. It reframes every output mojave produces into the language defense customers already speak. It creates a three-tier decision hierarchy (accept/reject automated; investigate requires human judgment; risk acceptance requires authority) that maps directly onto defense procurement workflows. And it connects mojave to NIST AI 800-3, the GUM, and the FY2026 NDAA AI governance requirements in a compliance story that competitors cannot replicate.

**2. Fix the confidence sequence pipeline (sigma=0.5 now, betting CS soon).**

This is the most urgent technical fix. The immediate patch is one line: replace Welford's estimated sigma with sigma=0.5 (the maximum standard deviation for Bernoulli data). Monte Carlo shows this restores coverage to >= 98.4% at all tested p values. The correct long-term solution is the Waudby-Smith & Ramdas 2024 betting confidence sequence for [0,1]-bounded data, which the project's own plan explicitly recommends but has not implemented. Additionally, the AnytimeMonitor must dispatch on DataFamily rather than ignoring it, and the eval-orchestrator's SequentialInstrument needs the same fix. A Gate 4 Monte Carlo test must be added that tests the actual production code path, not the function it happens to share a crate with.

**3. Activate G-theory from quarantine and use D-study projections to replace ad-hoc sample size planning.**

G-theory's D-study is a budget optimizer for Saltelli sampling. Instead of "run big, hope it's big enough, double if CIs are too wide (but don't actually check)," a pilot G-study estimates variance components and projects reliability at candidate sample sizes. For the current 6-factor design with 960 cells in a full factorial (cheaper than the current N=512 Saltelli's 4,096 cells), a D-study can answer: "you need N=1024 to achieve Phi >= 0.80" before the expensive Saltelli campaign runs. For defense customers paying per GPU-hour, this is a concrete cost-saving tool. The quarantined G-theory implementation is complete and tested; what is missing is the bridge that connects D-study projections to Saltelli N_base planning. Additionally, G-theory produces reliability coefficients (G and Phi) that Sobol indices cannot -- Sobol tells you what drives variance; G-theory tells you whether the resulting score is reliable enough to act on. Reporting both gives customers the full picture.

## Where This Project Should Go Next

The next conceptual move is not a feature. It is a reframing.

mojave has built the mathematical primitives. It has Sobol decomposition, confidence sequences, SPC charts, IRT, and an audit chain. What it lacks is the interpretive layer that connects these primitives to the measurement science they implement. The advisory revealed that this interpretive layer has a name: it is the construct validity dossier (BEAD-0011), and it is the capstone of the measurement-decision architecture that the advisory mapped:

1. **Item analysis** (Rasch fit, IRT calibration) establishes whether measurement is possible.
2. **Variance decomposition** (Sobol + G-theory) establishes what the measurement is actually measuring.
3. **Uncertainty quantification** (GUM + confidence sequences) establishes how precise the measurement is.
4. **Decision rules** (JCGM 106 + QMU) establish how to act on the measurement.
5. **Longitudinal monitoring** (SPC) establishes whether the measurement remains valid over time.
6. **Validity documentation** (construct validity dossier) establishes the warrant for trusting the measurement.

Each layer depends on the one above it, and each is gated by diagnostics from the previous layer. You do not run sensitivity analysis with an unqualified measurement system (MSA ndc < 3). You do not make conformity decisions without quantified uncertainty. You do not claim the benchmark measures reasoning if Sobol shows 85% of variance is prompt sensitivity.

The construct validity dossier assembles evidence from all layers: content validity (item pool coverage), structural validity (IRT model fit, factor structure), convergent and discriminant validity (MTMM CFA using the existing semopy infrastructure), sensitivity validity (the Construct Validity Index CVI = 1 - sum of Sobol indices for construct-irrelevant factors), and consequential validity (do high-scoring models actually perform well in deployment -- the ergodicity question that SPC monitoring answers). The dossier does not require new mathematical primitives. It requires the interpretive framework that makes existing primitives legible as measurement science.

The Construct Validity Index is the novel theoretical contribution that ties the project together. If CVI is defined as 1 minus the sum of first-order Sobol indices for factors the customer designates as construct-irrelevant, then the Sobol decomposition becomes a quantitative validity coefficient under Borsboom's causal definition. WMDP-bio's CVI of approximately 0.12 is a finding that no other tool can produce and no benchmark developer can ignore. It says, quantitatively and defensibly, that WMDP as currently administered is 88% a measure of how you ask the question and 12% a measure of what the model knows.

This is the argument that makes mojave matter. Not "we have better statistics" -- every framework claims better statistics. But "we can tell you, with a number, how much of your evaluation is actually measuring what you think it measures." That is measurement science. That is what defense customers need. That is what nobody else provides.

The immediate path: fix the CS pipeline (1 week), implement QMU + JCGM 106 (2 weeks), sign binaries with Sigstore (2 days), fix Python-Rust parity or retire the Python writer (1 week), activate G-theory (1 week), rerun WMDP at N=1024 with data quality gates and bare-prompt-separated reporting (2 weeks). The medium-term path: build the construct validity dossier, add Rasch fit diagnostics, implement MSA ndc/P-T, add ISO 5725 Mandel h/k, write the GSN assurance case template. The strategic path: publish the Sobol-as-validity-coefficient framing, establish QMU for AI evaluation as a concept the defense community recognizes, and position mojave as the measurement instrument that other instruments are calibrated against.

The project is closer to this than its authors may realize. The primitives are mature. The interpretive framework is mapped. The market window is open. What remains is the synthesis -- not in this document, but in the code, the run cards, and the customer conversations that follow.
